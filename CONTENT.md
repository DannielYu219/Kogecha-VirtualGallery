## 6. Why a "no license" rather than a Creative Commons license

The author of this site could have released the artwork under `CC BY-NC-ND 4.0` or similar. It was a deliberate choice **not** to do so, because:

1. The author is not the rights holder and therefore has no legal authority to attach **any** CC license to the works.
2. CC licenses are irrevocable once applied; choosing one on someone else's behalf would be presumptuous and potentially harmful to the actual rights holder.
3. "All rights reserved + an explicit courtesy list of permitted memorial uses" is a more honest and protective posture for this specific case.

The framework code, which the site author *does* own, is released under GPL-3.0 (see [`LICENSE`](./LICENSE)) so that anyone may fork, study, and improve the memorial site itself.
